COMMUNICATION SKILLS

Let's talk about our mood and feelings

How to express your emotions and feelings in English the way a native speaker would do it? Here are some colloquial idioms about different moods.

• Bent out of shape — to feel irritated, angry, losing your temper.
I was just trying to be nice, he had no reason to get bent out of shape.

• Tickled pink — to admire, to be delighted.
I was tickled pink when I got my Christmas gifts.

• Beat/Bushed — to feel very tired, to be exhausted.
I won’t go to the French lesson today, I’m totally bushed!

• Smth makes your day — what brings you joy.
I ate a really delicious danish, and it made my day.

• On pins and needles — to be anxious.
The news was so dreadful, she was on pins and needles all day!

• Sitting pretty — to be in a favorable position.
In five or six years time we will be sitting pretty, believe me.
